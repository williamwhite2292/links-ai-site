# Links AI Blog Strategy

This document defines the role of the blog / insights section on the `Links AI` website.

The blog is not filler content.
It is the proof layer for the business.

## 1. Purpose

The blog should do three jobs:

1. make `Links AI` feel real and thoughtful
2. show that we understand practical implementation, not just AI trends
3. bridge the gap between small workflow wins today and deeper AI systems later

## 2. Content Themes

### Theme A: practical first use cases

Examples:

- how to start with AI before your business is ready for agents
- common first workflow wins for service businesses
- where AI helps with follow-up, reporting, and classification

### Theme B: implementation thinking

Examples:

- what makes an AI workflow useful in the real world
- how to introduce AI into an existing custom software stack
- how to avoid buying tools before the workflow is clear

### Theme C: operating model

Examples:

- how Links AI runs its own work
- what visibility and accountability should look like in AI operations
- when a business should stay with a simple workflow and when it is ready for something deeper

## 4. Style Rules

The blog should:

- teach clearly
- use plain language
- stay grounded in real workflows
- avoid trend-chasing and hype framing
- show progression from simple to advanced

The blog should not:

- read like SEO sludge
- overuse AI buzzwords
- turn internal architecture into public jargon
- pretend every business needs the most advanced version immediately

## 5. Role in the Site

The blog supports the homepage by answering:

- “Are these people thoughtful?”
- “Do they understand real business workflows?”
- “Can they help me start small without boxing me in later?”

That is why the blog matters.
It is not just traffic content.
It is trust-building content.
